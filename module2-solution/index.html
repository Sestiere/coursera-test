Planets of our Solar System

Mercury
The smallest and the nearest to the Sun, Mercury is only slightly larger than the Moon.
  1. Distance from Sun: 58 million km
  2. One way light time to the Sun: ~ 3 mins
  3. Lenght of year: 88 days
  4. Planet type: Terrestrial
  5. Satellites: None
  6. Equatorial circumference: 15.000 km
  7. Density: 5.4 g/cm3
  8. Color: Grey
  9. Namesake: Roman god of Speed
Venus
The second planet from the Sun and Earth's closest planetary neighbor, Venus is the most similar planet to Earth.
  1. Distance from Sun: 108 million km
  2. One way light time to the Sun: ~ 6 mins
  3. Lenght of year: 225 days
  4. Planet type: Terrestrial
  5. Satellites: None
  6. Equatorial circumference: 38.000 km
  7. Density: 5.2 g/cm3
  8. Color: Red, Tan
  9. Namesake: Roman goddess of Love and Beauty
Earth
Our home planet is the third from the Sun, and the only place we wnow of so far that's inhabited by living things.
  1. Distance from Sun: 150 million km
  2. One way light time to the Sun: ~ 8 mins
  3. Lenght of year: 365 days
  4. Planet type: Terrestrial
  5. Satellites: 1 (the Moon)
  6. Equatorial circumference: 40.000 km
  7. Density: 5.5 g/cm3
  8. Color: Blue, White
  9. Namesake: Old English for 'Ground'
